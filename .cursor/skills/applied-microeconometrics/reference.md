# Applied Microeconometrics Reference

## Identification Checklist

- What is the estimand?
- What is the counterfactual?
- What assumptions connect the estimator to the estimand?
- What are the main threats to those assumptions?

## Common Design Families

- Difference-in-differences: timing, treatment variation, and parallel-trends logic.
- Instrumental variables: relevance, exclusion, monotonicity, and interpretation of the local effect.
- Regression discontinuity: running variable, bandwidth, manipulation, and local interpretation.
- Panel fixed effects: within-unit variation, treatment timing, and serial dependence.

## Inference Notes

- Match clustering to the design and treatment variation.
- Watch for small numbers of clusters or serial correlation problems.
- State whether standard errors, confidence intervals, and hypothesis tests are robust or conventional.

## Robustness Families

- Alternative samples and windows.
- Alternative controls or functional forms.
- Placebo outcomes, placebo timings, or falsification exercises.
- Sensitivity to coding, weighting, or bandwidth choices.

## Reporting Pattern

- Start with the estimand and design.
- Then report the preferred specification.
- Then summarize robustness and main threats to identification.
- End with a bounded interpretation rather than an over-general claim.
