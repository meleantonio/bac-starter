# Literature Review

## Research Question

This note reviews recent literature related to the baseline question in `spec/intent.md`:

> How do changes in key parameters of a baseline two-player continuous-time linear-quadratic differential game affect equilibrium strategies, state trajectories, and welfare-relevant outcomes?

## Scope And Search Notes

This review summarizes a selective search conducted on 2026-04-08 using built-in web tools and IDEAS/RePEc. The search focused on recent work from 2020-2025, with priority on economics journals and working-paper series, and with special attention to:

- continuous-time differential games
- feedback Nash or Markov-perfect equilibria
- comparative statics with respect to model parameters
- state trajectories and dynamic adjustment
- welfare, efficiency, and policy comparisons

This search pass did **not** surface recent direct matches in `Econometrica`, `AER`, `JPE`, `QJE`, or `ReStud`. The closest recent economics papers are concentrated in `Journal of Economic Theory`, `European Economic Review`, `Journal of Environmental Economics and Management`, `Journal of the Association of Environmental and Resource Economists`, `Journal of Public Economics`, and related field journals.

The strongest recent matches are mostly **application papers** in climate, common-pool resources, oligopoly, and networks rather than papers built around a canonical abstract linear-quadratic two-player benchmark. When evidence is partial, I say so directly.

## Main Takeaways

- Exact recent economics papers that match the baseline setup almost one-for-one are rare. The closest recent economics work tends to keep the continuous-time strategic structure while moving to applied settings such as climate agreements, commons, renewable-resource oligopoly, or criminal networks.[A dynamic analysis of criminal networks](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jetheo/v223y2025ics0022053124001571.html)[Too many or too few? On the optimal number of firms in the commons](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v121y2023ics0095069623000438.html)[A dynamic analysis of international environmental agreements under partial cooperation](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/eecrev/v143y2022ics0014292122000058.html)
- Feedback Nash or Markov-perfect reasoning remains central in the closest papers I found, including explicit feedback or Markov equilibrium analysis in criminal networks, renewable-resource oligopoly, dynamic pricing, common-pool resource taxation, and recent methods work.[A dynamic analysis of criminal networks](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jetheo/v223y2025ics0022053124001571.html)[Optimal taxation in a common resource oligopoly game](https://ideas.repec.org/a/kap/jeczfn/v129y2020i1d10.1007_s00712-019-00662-y.html)[Dynamic oligopoly pricing with reference-price effects](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/ejores/v288y2021i3p1006-1016.html)[Solving Nash Equilibria in Nonlinear Differential Games for Common-Pool Resources](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.06646v2)
- If the goal is a theory-first economics bibliography that still speaks to equilibrium strategies, state trajectories, and welfare, the best recent economics venues surfaced in this pass are `JET`, `EER`, `JEEM`, `JAERE`, and `JPubE`.[A dynamic analysis of criminal networks](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jetheo/v223y2025ics0022053124001571.html)[A dynamic analysis of international environmental agreements under partial cooperation](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/eecrev/v143y2022ics0014292122000058.html)[Climate Change Impact on Economic Growth: Regional Climate Policy under Cooperation and Noncooperation](https://ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jaerec/doi10.1086-722274.html)[A simple dynamic climate cooperation model](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/pubeco/v194y2021ics0047272720301936.html)
- If the goal is a recent methods bridge back toward a more canonical continuous-time differential-game benchmark, the most useful open papers from this search are the 2025 working papers by Boucekkine et al. and Cai, Xepapadeas, and de Zeeuw.[Differential climate games with heterogenous players](https://ideas.repec.org/p/aim/wpaimx/2534.html)[Solving Nash Equilibria in Nonlinear Differential Games for Common-Pool Resources](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.06646v2)

## Priority Reading List

### 1. [A dynamic analysis of criminal networks](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jetheo/v223y2025ics0022053124001571.html)

Venue: `Journal of Economic Theory`, 2025.

Why it matters: This is the closest top-theory-journal paper I found. It explicitly derives a Markov Feedback Equilibrium, studies comparative dynamics with respect to network size, network density, and marginal expected punishment, and also analyzes planner targeting.[A dynamic analysis of criminal networks](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jetheo/v223y2025ics0022053124001571.html)

Access: The journal version is paywalled on ScienceDirect, but IDEAS links several postprints and working-paper versions, including a HAL postprint.[A dynamic analysis of criminal networks](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jetheo/v223y2025ics0022053124001571.html)[HAL postprint](https://ideas.repec.org/p/hal/pseptp/hal-04850675.html)

### 2. [Too many or too few? On the optimal number of firms in the commons](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v121y2023ics0095069623000438.html)

Venue: `Journal of Environmental Economics and Management`, 2023.

Why it matters: The paper studies common-pool non-renewable resource industries in an infinite-horizon differential game with either open-loop or feedback strategies. It is highly relevant for equilibrium/efficiency comparisons because it derives conditions under which industry size is socially optimal and studies regulation through license fees.[Too many or too few? On the optimal number of firms in the commons](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v121y2023ics0095069623000438.html)

Access: The JEEM version is paywalled, but IDEAS links a HAL postprint.[Too many or too few? On the optimal number of firms in the commons](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v121y2023ics0095069623000438.html)[HAL postprint](https://ideas.repec.org/p/hal/journl/hal-04207035.html)

### 3. [A dynamic analysis of international environmental agreements under partial cooperation](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/eecrev/v143y2022ics0014292122000058.html)

Venue: `European Economic Review`, 2022.

Why it matters: This paper is a strong economics match on coalition dynamics, dynamic equilibrium participation, and welfare. It shows that coalition size can increase over time even from a small initial coalition and compares narrow-but-deep agreements with broad-but-shallow agreements in welfare and Pareto terms.[A dynamic analysis of international environmental agreements under partial cooperation](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/eecrev/v143y2022ics0014292122000058.html)

Access: The journal version is paywalled, but a CIRANO working-paper version is linked.[A dynamic analysis of international environmental agreements under partial cooperation](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/eecrev/v143y2022ics0014292122000058.html)[CIRANO working paper](https://ideas.repec.org/p/cir/cirwor/2022s-01.html)

### 4. [Climate Change Impact on Economic Growth: Regional Climate Policy under Cooperation and Noncooperation](https://ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jaerec/doi10.1086-722274.html)

Venue: `Journal of the Association of Environmental and Resource Economists`, 2023.

Why it matters: This paper is less canonical in form than the baseline question but very strong on welfare. It compares cooperation and noncooperation in a dynamic climate setting, computes regional social costs of carbon, and highlights differential welfare losses across regions, especially in the tropics and in the absence of transfers.[Climate Change Impact on Economic Growth: Regional Climate Policy under Cooperation and Noncooperation](https://ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jaerec/doi10.1086-722274.html)

Access: The JAERE version is subscription-gated, but a DEOS working-paper version is linked on IDEAS.[Climate Change Impact on Economic Growth: Regional Climate Policy under Cooperation and Noncooperation](https://ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jaerec/doi10.1086-722274.html)[DEOS working paper](https://ideas.repec.org/p/aue/wpaper/2214.html)

### 5. [On breadth and depth of climate agreements with pledge-and-review](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v125y2024ics0095069624000263.html)

Venue: `Journal of Environmental Economics and Management`, 2024.

Why it matters: This paper studies dynamic games in which countries emit, invest in green technology, decide on coalition participation, and negotiate contract duration. It is useful for welfare and partial-cooperation comparisons because it shows when narrow-but-deep agreements can dominate broad-but-shallow ones and when high cooperation can support first-best outcomes.[On breadth and depth of climate agreements with pledge-and-review](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v125y2024ics0095069624000263.html)

Access: I verified abstract-level access through IDEAS; the JEEM publisher version is paywalled, and I did not confirm an open paper version during this pass.[On breadth and depth of climate agreements with pledge-and-review](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v125y2024ics0095069624000263.html)

### 6. [Product quality differentiation in a renewable resource oligopoly](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v111y2022ics0095069621001303.html)

Venue: `Journal of Environmental Economics and Management`, 2022.

Why it matters: This is one of the cleanest recent papers for parameter-driven welfare comparative statics. It introduces a parameter for resource quality and studies how changes in that parameter affect exploitation and social welfare, with the notable result that higher resource quality can be welfare-reducing.[Product quality differentiation in a renewable resource oligopoly](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v111y2022ics0095069621001303.html)

Access: The JEEM version is paywalled. I did not verify an open paper version during this pass.[Product quality differentiation in a renewable resource oligopoly](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v111y2022ics0095069621001303.html)

### 7. [On the profitability of cross-ownership in Cournot nonrenewable resource oligopolies: Stock size matters](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v111y2022ics0095069621001388.html)

Venue: `Journal of Environmental Economics and Management`, 2022.

Why it matters: This paper is a useful dynamic-state complement for the baseline question because its strategic conclusions depend on initial resource stocks. It shows that symmetric cross-ownership can be profitable when stocks are small enough and discusses the competition-policy implications.[On the profitability of cross-ownership in Cournot nonrenewable resource oligopolies: Stock size matters](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v111y2022ics0095069621001388.html)

Access: The JEEM version is paywalled, but working-paper versions are linked through CESifo and CIRANO.[On the profitability of cross-ownership in Cournot nonrenewable resource oligopolies: Stock size matters](https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jeeman/v111y2022ics0095069621001388.html)[CESifo working paper](https://ideas.repec.org/p/ces/ceswps/_8503.html)[CIRANO working paper](https://ideas.repec.org/p/cir/cirwor/2020s-43.html)

### 8. [Optimal taxation in a common resource oligopoly game](https://ideas.repec.org/a/kap/jeczfn/v129y2020i1d10.1007_s00712-019-00662-y.html)

Venue: `Journal of Economics`, 2020.

Why it matters: This is one of the closest matches to the canonical equilibrium object in the project. It studies a differential oligopoly game over a renewable resource and shows that, in a Feedback Nash Equilibrium, a linear Markov tax that is affine in the resource stock can decentralize the socially optimal path of output.[Optimal taxation in a common resource oligopoly game](https://ideas.repec.org/a/kap/jeczfn/v129y2020i1d10.1007_s00712-019-00662-y.html)

Access: I verified access to the abstract page through IDEAS. I did not confirm an unrestricted full-text PDF from the publisher during this pass.[Optimal taxation in a common resource oligopoly game](https://ideas.repec.org/a/kap/jeczfn/v129y2020i1d10.1007_s00712-019-00662-y.html)

### 9. [Differential climate games with heterogenous players](https://ideas.repec.org/p/aim/wpaimx/2534.html)

Venue: `AMSE Working Papers`, 2025.

Why it matters: This is one of the best recent working-paper matches for methodology. It studies a two-country dynamic game with heterogeneity in preferences, discounting, and damages; computes Nash equilibria and planner benchmarks; and then compares emissions, damages, consumption, transfers, and welfare under heterogeneity and Knightian uncertainty.[Differential climate games with heterogenous players](https://ideas.repec.org/p/aim/wpaimx/2534.html)

Access: The working-paper page links an open PDF.[Differential climate games with heterogenous players](https://ideas.repec.org/p/aim/wpaimx/2534.html)

### 10. [Solving Nash Equilibria in Nonlinear Differential Games for Common-Pool Resources](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.06646v2)

Venue: `arXiv`, 2025.

Why it matters: This is the most useful recent open methods paper I found. It develops new numerical methods for open-loop and feedback Nash equilibria in nonlinear differential games and applies them to one- and two-dimensional common-pool resource problems, with special emphasis on the lake game.[Solving Nash Equilibria in Nonlinear Differential Games for Common-Pool Resources](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.06646v2)

Access: Open access on arXiv.[Solving Nash Equilibria in Nonlinear Differential Games for Common-Pool Resources](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.06646v2)

## Bottom Line

The literature search supports a clear conclusion: recent economics work closest to the project's baseline question exists, but it is dispersed across dynamic applications rather than packaged as an abstract canonical LQ benchmark.
